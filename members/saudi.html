Saudi Arabia
About The State

Saudi Arabia is a sparsely populated kingdom located in the Middle East, extending across most of the northern and central Arabian Peninsula. Despite its youth as a nation, Saudi Arabia is heir to a rich history. The Hejaz region, located along the Red Sea in the western highlands, is the cradle of Islam and home to the religion's holiest cities, Mecca and Medina. The vast arid zone in the geographic heartland, known as Najd or "Highland," was until recently populated by nomadic tribes. Meanwhile, along the Persian Gulf to the east, lies the country's abundant oil fields that have made Saudi Arabia synonymous with petroleum wealth since the 1960s. These three elements – religion, tribalism, and vast wealth – have played a major role in the country's history.
